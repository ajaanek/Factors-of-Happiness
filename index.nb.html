Factors of Happiness

by Ajaane Kanagasbai

Introduction

The data set I used is the World Happiness Report from Kaggle, specifically the 2019 data. I chose this data set to try to find out the answer to the age-old question: what is the key to happiness? This data set includes the happiness scores and rankings from the Gallup World Poll, as well as different potential factors such as GDP per capita, healthy life expectancy, trust in government, freedom, social support, etc [2]. Using this information, I will attempt to find which factors are most important for living a happy life so that individuals can focus on improving what will most likely make them happy. To do this, I will find out which parts of the world are the most happy, then any potential factors that might contribute to their higher happiness.

I must note that the data is not entirely accurate. There are concerns due to the metrics and collection of data [2]. Metrics are different across the globe and can be influenced by things such as the way their countrys education system grades assessments. In addition, data collection is not equally distributed among continents so that may skew the results.

Meanwhile, generosity is the least correlated with happiness. with that in mind Lets see how each of these factors are reflected in the happiness score of each continent:

avg_by_continent <- data |> select(-Score, -Rank, -Country_or_Region) |> group_by(Continent) |> summarise_if(is.numeric, mean)

ggplot(avg_melt, aes(y=value, x=Continent, color=Continent, fill=Continent)) + geom_bar( stat="identity") + facet_wrap(~variable) + theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 90)) + labs(title = "Average of Each Factor by Continent", y = "Average Value", subtitle = "Fig. 5") + scale_fill_viridis_d() + theme(plot.title = element_text(face = "bold.italic", hjust = 0.5, size=12), axis.title.x = element_text(vjust=-1), axis.ticks = element_line(colour = "grey70", linewidth = 0.2), plot.background = element_rect(fill="linen", colour="black", linewidth = 2), panel.grid.minor = element_line(colour = "gray"), panel.grid.major = element_blank(), legend.key = element_rect( colour = "linen"), legend.key.width = unit(0.75, "cm"), legend.key.height = unit(0.75, "cm"), legend.title = element_text(face="bold", size = 8), legend.background = element_rect(fill="linen", colour = "grey50", size = 1))

We can see that Oceania is leading on all factors. This makes sense considering Oceania has the highest happiness score. Europe is second for almost all factors. The Americas is higher than Asia in social support, life expectancy, and freedom, while Asia is higher in Generosity and trust in government. If all factors influenced happiness by the same amount, you might expect Asia and the Americas happiness scores to more similar. Since generosity and trust in government impact happiness less, the gap between the two widens. Africa is rated the lowest on almost all factors, contributing to the lowest happiness score.

Conclusion

From analyzing the data, we could see that Oceania and Europe have some of the happiest populations in the world. Some of the contributing factors are: GDP per capita, Life Expectancy and Social Support.
